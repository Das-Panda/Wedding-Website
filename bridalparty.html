  Kristen - Matron of honor, best friend for life: I have known Kristen since we were about 5 years old when we met in a school program called "Mother's Day Out" at Carrollton Christian Academy. We initially hated each other as little girls and had to pretend to like each other when the school photographer took a picture of us on the playground… forced to hug by the slide (Kristen's mom has the newspaper article). We did the multiple night slumber parties (Friday night, Saturday night) all throughout grade school and middle school either at my house or hers. We shared our sixteenth birthdays together and were surprised by both of our families who had planned a country and western dance party out in the middle of nowhere! Kristen started driving before me, so we have many memories of going to the movie theaters and malls ourselves all on our own! Although we went to separate high schools, we still remained close, even when she finished her senior year in Tennessee - I was so excited to be there for her graduation! College took me even further away from her and my trips to see her became less even non-existent except for holidays/July 4ths when we both happened to be in Dallas at the same time. Kristen never judged me or got upset with me for not calling and catching up as often as best friends should – she accepts me and all my faults. Kristen has such a big heart for her family and serves as a great role model, especially for her younger sister, brother, and cousin, Ron. Kristen knows my past in terms of school, play, and love and her support throughout all these years has kept me afloat spiritually and mentally. Her words of advice during some of the difficult times in my life were exactly what I needed to hear. She remains to be a woman I can trust, lean on, and confide in. Kristen and I have a special kind of friendship, the most unique of friendships really - a relationship that can survive the test of time and distance. We share some pretty amazing memories and I plan to make many many more as the years pass. She met Kenneth for the first time July 4th, 2008. Her support for the relationship Kenneth and I share means so much to me, and Kenneth and I constantly look up to her and her husband for an example of a forever kind of love.

She was there for me during the hard, stressful times of college, and never stopped supporting me. I've learned so much from Emily – everything from learning to search better on Google to learning how to show my faith in the Lord to others. Although we share many things in common, Emily has a bigger heart than I do and puts others first before herself more frequently than I do. Emily met Kenneth for the first time during the fall of 2008, and Kenneth spent Christmas 2008 dinner with Emily and her family. She has continually supported my relationship with Kenneth and always provides me with sound advice when it comes to handling my relationship with Kenneth and my relationship with myself.

Taking trips to see Tiffany in college were really special for me – getting to see my sister in her college environment and sharing in her daily routine was a great experience and preparation for dorm life. As I started college, Tiffany made trips to see me during holidays and other times during the year as well, and getting to share that part of my life with her in San Diego was exciting for me. Tiffany is slightly messier than I am. Tiffany is a better runner/athlete than me. Tiffany communicates emotions and all things lovey dovey and I do not. Tiffany reads more/quicker than I do. Despite all of our differences, we have one common denominator: our desire to follow Christ in life, love, and play. Tiffany met Kenneth for the first time the weekend before my mother got married in June 2008. Her encouragement she provides for my relationship with Kenneth is a gift I do not take for granted.
